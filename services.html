Directors

Oscar Denis

Director of Bands

Oscar Denis is the Director of Bands at R.L. Turner High School. He graduated from Stephen F. Austin State University with his Bachelors in Music Education While at SFA, he was part of several organizations including the Lumberjack Marching Band, the Roarin’ Buzzsaw Basketball band, and the Swingin’ Aces Jazz band. As a member of the LMB, Mr. Denis was a Trumpet Section leader. He was also a member of the Wind Symphony under the direction of Dr. David W. Campo and the Wind Ensemble under the direction of Mr. Fred J. Allen.

Before teaching at R.L. Turner as an assistant director, Mr. Denis taught in Lubbock, Texas where he was the middle school Director of Bands at Dunbar College Preparatory Academy conducting the Honors band, as well as assisting with the Concert band and Jazz band. While at Dunbar, his band earned UIL Superior ratings in Concert and Sight-Reading contest, which was the first ever in the school’s history. Prior to Lubbock, Mr. Denis also served as the Director of Bands at Forest Park Middle School in Longview, TX, where he grew the band program from around 50 students to 300 before leaving. 

Mr. Denis’s professional affiliations include: Texas Music Educators Association, Texas Bandmasters Association, and Kappa Kappa Psi, National Honorary Band Fraternity. 

Mr. Denis is married to Dr. Andrea Denis, and they have a young daughter named Maddie. His wife, Andrea, is currently the horn professor at Texas Woman’s University in Denton and horn instructor for Coppell ISD. On his free time, Mr. Denis enjoys cooking and hanging out with his family.

JaRod Hall

Assistant Director

Mr. Hall is returning home to join the R. L. Turner band staff for the 2019-2020 school year! Previously he served as Associate Director of Bands at Griffin Middle School in Frisco, TX where he directed the Symphonic Band and GatorJazz. His ensembles are perennial sweepstakes winners; his ensemble in 2017-18 was awarded the Citation of Excellence, honoring the top two Non-Varsity middle school bands in the state of Texas, Class A.

Mr. Hall earned his Bachelor of Music Degree from the University of North Texas. As a tuba and bass trombone player at UNT, he performed with the North Texas Wind Symphony under the direction of Eugene Corporon, the Symphonic Band under the direction of Dennis Fisher, the 3 O'Clock Lab Band under the direction of Brad Leali, and UNT Grammy-Nominated Funky Fresh Basketball Band under the direction of Nicholas Williams. He was also a four-year member of the UNT Green Brigade Marching Band, serving as a drum major his last two years. Mr. Hall spent several summers performing professionally; in 2013 he served as drum major for the Crossmen Drum and Bugle Corps; in 2014 he performed with the Disneyland All-American College Band. The All-American College Band (AACB) is a 21-member jazz-influenced outdoor performing ensemble comprised of the top college musicians in the country. While Mr. Hall was one of two tubas selected for the band, he also performed with the AACB on bass trombone and vocals.

Mr. Hall is also an active composer and arranger. His arrangements have been performed by each of the UNT Athletic Bands, the UNT Tuba Euphonium Ensembles, the Griffin Bands, Boswell HS, Wakeland HS, and the Disneyland AACB. His compositions have received critical acclaim and have been performed by the Carrollton Wind Symphony and the Wakeland cluster in Frisco, TX. His most recent publication, Lost Woods Fantasy, will be performed at the Midwest Clinic and Convention this December by the Berkner HS Symphonic Band I.

Raised in Carrollton, Mr. Hall is a graduate of R. L. Turner HS. He is a four-year All-State musician; the first three years, he was selected to the TMEA 5A Symphonic Band; his final year, he ranked first chair in the state of Texas for bass trombone in the TMEA Jazz Ensemble. Mr. Hall also participated in the R. L. Turner Full Orchestra, Theater, and Mu Alpha Theta math honor society. Professionally, Mr. Hall is affiliated with Texas Music Educators Association, Phi Mu Alpha Sinfonia, the Millennium Composers Initiative, The FJH Publishing Company, and the Carrollton Wind Symphony.

Mr. Hall resides in Grapevine with his fiancee, Rachel, his tuba, Sylvester, and his trombone, Lucile.

Josh Dominguez

Director of Percussion

dominguezj@cfbisd.edu

Josh Dominguez has served as the Director of Percussion for the RL Turner High School Band since 2016, coordinating all marching and concert percussion activities. Prior to being at RL Turner, he served as an Assistant Band Director and Director of Percussion at Lubbock High School in Lubbock, TX.

Mr. Dominguez holds his Bachelor’s degree in Music Education from the University of North Texas. During his time at UNT, he taught at various schools in the DFW area. He served as a battery instructor at Marcus High School, assisting in their 2014 Marching show and Drumline Show productions. He also taught at Byron Nelson High School, as a battery instructor and private lesson teacher. Mr. Dominguez has also served as a member of the UNT Marching Percussion Camp staff.

As a performer, Mr. Dominguez marched snare drum for the Boston Crusaders Drum and Bugle Corps in 2012 and 2013.

At UNT, he performed as a member of the UNT Snare Line, Symphony Orchestra, as well as showcased performances with the UNT Percussion Players and 2 o’ Clock Steel Band.

Mr. Dominguez studied percussion with Mark Ford, Christopher Deane, Paul Rennick, Ed Smith, and Jose Aponte. He also studied percussion arranging and literature with Paul Rennick. Mr. Dominguez has provided percussion arranging for groups in the DFW and Lubbock area.

Mr. Dominguez is an active member of the Texas Music Educators Association and Percussive Arts Society.
